## Later (v2 — queued)

- **Historical track-record view** — month/quarter scorecards, by-scout hit rates, time-series of hit-rate.
- **Per-scout breakdown surfacing** — when a pick hits or misses, attribute the outcome back to which scout was most predictive.
- **Multi-chain.** Same rubric, different launch flows. Base + Hyperliquid first, probably.
- **API rate limits + keys** — once the daily-call audience is big enough that anonymous bursts become a problem.
- **Custom rubrics for holders.** Tweak the weights, save a personal lens on the daily shortlist.

## Deliberately out of scope

- **Auto-trading / a buy button.** Meridian is research, not a brokerage. Not now, not ever.
- **A "buy this token" recommendation.** Every pick is *worth investigating*, never *buy*. That phrase doesn't show up anywhere in the product, and won't.
- **Closed-source scoring.** The rubric is public so it's auditable. If it ever becomes a black box, the track record stops being trustworthy.
- **A token utility that holders have to spend.** Holding is the gate. There's no burn-to-access, no in-product currency. `$MRDN` accrues value from the credibility of the call — not from being consumed.

## Versioning

The rubric is versioned (`v3` at time of writing). Changes that materially affect scores will bump the version and be noted in [the changelog](https://github.com/Enoch208/Meridian/blob/main/CHANGELOG.md) (when shipped). The honesty rules in [`honesty-model`](honesty-model) are stable — they're the contract.
