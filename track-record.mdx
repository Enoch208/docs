title: "The track record"
description: "Every call logged with a timestamp and score. Wins and misses both shown."
---

The track record is the moat. A one-shot due-diligence tool can claim it's honest, but it can never *prove* it — there's no record of how its past calls played out. Meridian's record is **honest by construction**: append-only log + scorecard derived on every read.

## How it works

Every pick the swarm surfaces is written to a permanent log (`calls.jsonl` on disk in tests, **MongoDB Atlas** in production). Each record:

| Field | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `date` | `"2026-05-25"` | When the call was made |
| `rank` | `1` | Which pick on that day (1 = top) |
| `token.name / symbol / address` | `"Pulse" / "PULSE" / "Pu1s…"` | Identity at call-time |
| `score_at_call` | `84` | Composite score at surfacing time |
| `price_at_call_usd` | `0.00041` | Entry price |
| `price_now_usd` | `0.00086` | Filled by the update job (DexScreener) |
| `pct_change` | `109.8` | Computed: `(now - at_call) / at_call × 100` |
| `status` | `"hit"` | `open` until decided; then `hit` / `miss` |

The log is **append-only.** No edit, no delete. The scorecard is **derived** from the log on every API read.

## Outcome rules

The rules are fixed and documented:

```
pct_change = (price_now − price_at_call) / price_at_call × 100

if pct_change ≥ +50%   →  hit
if pct_change ≤ −50%   →  miss
otherwise              →  open
```

A pick stays `open` until it crosses one of those thresholds. Some calls stay open for weeks — that's normal, and the scorecard shows them honestly.

<Note>
  Hit/miss thresholds are intentionally **symmetric and wide**. A 50% move is meaningful; a 10% move is noise. Anything in between stays `open` — we don't claim a win on a chop.
</Note>

## Verify it yourself

The whole point is that you don't have to trust us. Three checks anyone can run:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pull the raw scorecard">
    ```bash
    curl https://meridian-backend-qae0.onrender.com/api/track-record | python3 -m json.tool
    ```
    Every call we've made shows up. Hits and misses both.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reproduce a hit / miss yourself">
    Take any call from the list. Re-fetch its price from DexScreener. Apply the formula:
    ```
    pct_change = (current_price − price_at_call_usd) / price_at_call_usd × 100
    ```
    Your answer should match the `pct_change` on the record (within tick rounding).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the source">
    The outcome rules live in [`backend/meridian/trackrecord/update.py`](https://github.com/Enoch208/Meridian/blob/main/backend/meridian/trackrecord/update.py). The append-only invariant lives in [`store.py`](https://github.com/Enoch208/Meridian/blob/main/backend/meridian/trackrecord/store.py). Both are ~80 lines, both testable, both in the open.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why this matters

Hold-to-unlock for `$MRDN` only works if the call is worth holding for. The track record is what makes that call worth holding for — every day's pick is a deposit into a credibility account that compounds.

If we make a bad call, it shows up. We can't selectively show wins because the log isn't editable and the scorecard isn't computed by us — it's derived. That's the deal.
