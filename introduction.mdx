## The thesis

Hundreds of tokens launch on Solana every day; the overwhelming majority are noise or traps. The hard part for a trader isn't evaluating a token they've *already found* — it's **finding the few worth evaluating in the first place**, fast, before the move.

Most existing agents answer *"is this token I already brought safe?"* Meridian answers the question that comes before it: **"which tokens should I even be looking at right now?"** That single shift — from reactive evaluation to proactive discovery — is the product.

## What it is, in one paragraph

A swarm of specialized scout agents that continuously scans new Solana token launches, scores each against a transparent rubric, and surfaces the few worth investigating today — with a **public, running track record** of every call. Every signal that can be verified is graded; every signal that can't is marked Unknown and down-weighted — never invented.

## What makes it different

| | One-shot due-diligence | **Meridian** |
|---|---|---|
| **Job** | Evaluate a token you bring | Discover tokens you didn't know about |
| **Stance** | Defensive — "is it safe?" | Offensive — "where's the edge?" |
| **Cadence** | One-shot, on demand | Daily, recurring |
| **Memory** | None | Public, running track record |
| **Reason to return** | Weak — use once, flip | Strong — the next call is tomorrow |
| **Viral engine** | A single screenshot | A track record that compounds |

The **track record** is the moat — it's the thing a paste-a-token verdict structurally can't have. An honest record of wins *and* misses, in public, is simultaneously the credibility moat, the retention hook, and the viral engine.
